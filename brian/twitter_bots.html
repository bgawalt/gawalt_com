Brian's Twitter Bots

A bunch of bots I made for Twitter. They're all on hiatus right now because I'm upset with how that website's been moderated since late 2022. TODO!

  • Marion's Wish
In order of how much I like them:
  • Each Wordsquare: Tweets N-by-N grids of letters that spell the same words left-to-right as they do top to bottom. [github] (Mar 2017)

  • #ReaderBot: This bot pulled from a manually-maintained spreadsheet to sporadically tweet what book I was currently reading and when I was likely to finish it. [github] (Nov 2016)

  • Synthetic Shakespeare: I trained a Markov model on the digram transitions of the works of Shakespeare, and now I can sample new Shakespeare-sounding verses. (July 2010)

  • RandomBabel: Another Markov bot, trained on the King James Version of the baby. (July 2010)

  • 1-900-BAD-SEXT A bot that goes out and finds present-tense tweets, then repeats them after the famous "SEXT:" prefix. So long, context! [github] (Oct 2015)

  • Acrostician: A bot that kept a running count of how often n-grams appear on Twitter, then tries to patch together a reasonable sounding acrostic which matches a specified target. Its account is currently suspended! [github] (Jun 2014)
