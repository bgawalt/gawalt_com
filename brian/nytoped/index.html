These words are automatically identified as having a conspicuously high or conspicuously low probability of use within a paragraph from the given author, as compared to usage probabilities across all other authors. The scores are drawn from a logarithmic scale of these probabilities, where a score of 1 indicates the word is 10 times more likely to appear in a paragraph from the author in question than in a paragraph drawn at random from the opinion page (and similarly, a score of -1 indicates the word is 10 times less likely to appear in a paragraph from the author).
